Project 01

Sport Performance: Barefoot running as a neuromuscular recalibration

I am investigating whether a brief barefoot running session can act as a "neuromuscular reset" to improve running mechanics and reduce injury risk factors in both recreational and experienced runners.

I designed a multimodal data pipeline in Python and MATLAB to synchronize high-frequency EMG signals, 3D motion capture, and force plate kinetics. I am currently processing these inputs through OpenSim musculoskeletal models to isolate internal joint moments and identify subtle recalibration patterns in motor control.

By applying PCA and time-series analysis, I aim to map how the CNS adjusts to the removal of shoe constraints. The goal is to establish a protocol that helps runners understand and optimize their movement patterns to stay injury-free using their natural running gait.

Project 02

AI & Healthcare Hackathon ⎟ Finalist Mistral AI x GE Healthcare

Radiologists often spend too much time reviewing manually patient history and previous scans to draft current tumor reports, creating significant bottlenecks in the clinical workflow. For this hackathon, our goal was to build an AI-driven system that would assist radiologists in drafting MRI tumor reports while integrating easily into their existing routine.

Over 48 hours, competing against 23 teams, we designed and built a multi-agent AI workflow based on a long- and short-term memory and DICOM MRI scans. To differentiate our solution, I integrated a RAG pipeline strictly grounded in RECIST 1.1 medical guidelines. To guarantee a zero-hallucination environment for clinical use, we forced the LLM to output its responses exclusively in a structured JSON format, ensuring data integrity and consistency.

We successfully bridged the gap between agentic AI capabilities and strict medical compliance. Our app earned us a spot in the Top 8.
