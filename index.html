Sport Performance: Barefoot Running as a Neuromuscular Recalibration

I am investigating whether a brief barefoot running session can act as a "neuromuscular reset" to improve running mechanics and reduce injury risk factors in both recreational and experienced runners.

I designed a multimodal data pipeline in Python and MATLAB to synchronize high-frequency EMG signals, 3D motion capture, and force plate kinetics. I am currently processing these inputs through OpenSim musculoskeletal models to isolate internal joint moments and identify subtle recalibration patterns in motor control.

By applying PCA and time-series analysis, I aim to map how the CNS adjusts to the removal of shoe constraints, with the goal of establishing a protocol that helps runners optimize their movement patterns and stay injury-free using their natural gait.

AI & Healthcare Hackathon Finalist · Mistral AI × GE Healthcare

Radiologists often spend too much time reviewing patient history and previous scans to draft tumor reports, creating significant bottlenecks in clinical workflow. Our goal was to build an AI-driven system that assists radiologists in drafting MRI tumor reports while integrating easily into their existing routine.

Over 48 hours, competing against 23 teams, we designed and built a multi-agent AI workflow with long- and short-term memory and DICOM MRI scan processing. I integrated a RAG pipeline strictly grounded in RECIST 1.1 medical guidelines. To guarantee a zero-hallucination environment for clinical use, we forced the LLM to output responses exclusively in structured JSON format.

We successfully bridged the gap between agentic AI capabilities and strict medical compliance, earning a spot in the Top 8.
