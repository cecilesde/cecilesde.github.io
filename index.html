Project 01

Neuromuscular Reset Analysis

Traditional biomechanical studies on barefoot running often lack high-fidelity temporal tracking to understand how quickly the Central Nervous System (CNS) recalibrates motor control after removing shoe constraints.

To capture this, I designed a robust data pipeline using Python and MATLAB to synchronize high-frequency EMG signals, 3D optical motion capture data, and force plate kinetics. I then processed these raw inputs into an OpenSim musculoskeletal model to simulate internal joint moments.

This approach allowed me to successfully map the error-based learning curve, providing quantitative biomechanical evidence of neuromuscular reset and establishing a replicable framework for future sensorimotor learning research.

Project 02

Agentic AI for Oncology Reporting

Hospital oncology departments face massive bottlenecks in MRI reporting, often relying on traditional FIFO (First-In-First-Out) queues rather than prioritizing by clinical urgency and tumor progression.

For the GE Healthcare and Mistral AI Hackathon, our team had 48 hours to build an AI-driven triaging and reporting system that physicians could trust, strictly adhering to RECIST 1.1 medical guidelines. While part of the team focused on the core LLM fine-tuning, I anchored the clinical logic—ensuring the ML architecture outputted structured, hallucination-free narratives.

We successfully engineered a multi-agent RAG workflow integrated with an XGBoost Learning-to-Rank system for the triage queue. This proof-of-concept bridged the gap between raw LLM capabilities and strict medical compliance, earning us a spot in the Top 8 out of 23 competing teams.

Project 03

Single-Molecule Tracking Pipeline

In fluorescence microscopy applied to Parkinson's disease research, analyzing particle movement at the nanometer scale is critical. However, conventional equipment often suffers from low Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR), making localization extremely challenging.

I built a complete computer vision pipeline from scratch to address this. The solution utilized OpenCV and Python, incorporating advanced signal processing and 2D Gaussian fitting to achieve subpixel refinement of the molecules' positions.

This upgraded pipeline significantly enhanced the precision of our diagnostic equipment, enabling the research team to reliably track single-molecule dynamics under conditions where standard tracking software failed.
